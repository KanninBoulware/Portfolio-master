Standard #10: Leadership and Collaboration

The teacher seeks appropriate leadership roles and opportunities to take responsibility for student learning, to collaborate with learners, families, colleagues, other school professionals, and community members to ensure learner growth, and to advance the profession (InTASC, 2013).

Brief Description of Evidence: In EDUC 240, Introduction to Physical and Health Education for Elementary Teachers which was completed in the spring of 2022, I made a healthy snack and activity calendar to send home with the students so that they can work the activities as well as healthy snacks into their daily routine. It also allows for the students and families/communities to be able to do activities together. The calendar is also used so that students can visualize what healthy habits can look like laid out across a calendar.


Analysis of What I Learned: This artifact has taught me different types of ways of including parents and the community in the students educational journey. This artifact has allowed me to learn the importance of healthy habits along with forming healthy habits with younger children.


How This Artifact Demonstrates My Competence in the InTASC Standard: This artifact demonstrates my competence because it shows how in depth I am willing to go to get the students' parents and/or communities involved with my students outside of the classroom.
